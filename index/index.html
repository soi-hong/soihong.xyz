Soi Hong's practice explores language, visual structures, and systems of classification through drawing, graphic design, and artist book production. Her work deconstructs institutional forms—government documents, library markings, linguistic catalogs—reassembling them into visual formats. Key projects include Regulation (analyzing South Korean document structures), Important (transforming underlined poetry passages into drawings), and What happened? (diagramming looted museum objects' trajectories). Her background in visual communication design and traditional painting informs her experimental approach to typography, repetition, and categorization, exploring structural relationships between language and image through artist books, exhibitions, and publications.
