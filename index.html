I am a graphic designer, collector, classifier, drawing practitioner, and artist. I hold an MA in Visual Communication Design from the University of Seoul and an MA and BA in Oriental Painting from Sungshin Women's University. My practice explores the intersection of drawing, language, and systems of organization. I have held three solo exhibitions, including Cylinder Circle (2024, plot room) and If it is good (2022, Alltimespace), and participated in over fifteen group exhibitions both online and offline, covering themes ranging from typographic practice to curatorial experimentation. As the founder of NOWWE Publishing and Sculpture Poetry, I create artist books that function as sculptural and poetic objects. My publications have been featured at the Tokyo Art Book Fair and the Unlimited Edition Seoul Art Book Fair annually since 2019. My design work includes illustration and graphic design for publications, symposiums, and cultural platforms. Notable projects include visual design for the Hwanghak Kitchen Design Fair and illustrations for Amnesty International Korea’s human rights publication 50+ Voices. Through publishing, exhibitions, and workshops, I investigate how visual structures—such as classification systems, typographic forms, and printed matter—can carry personal, collective, and poetic meaning.
